About Coding

We are Senior High School students studying Information Technology (IT). In IT, coding is an important skill that allows us to create programs, websites, and systems using programming languages. Coding is the process of writing instructions that tell a computer what to do. Through coding, we can develop useful applications, manage data, and build technology solutions that help people and organizations. As an IT student, We are continuously learning different programming languages and improving our coding skills to become a future IT professional.

Credits

Coding has come a long way, thanks to many amazing people and their ideas. Here are some of the most important contributors to the world of coding:

  • Charles Babbage: He is called the "Father of the Computer" because he designed the first mechanical computer in the 1800s.
  • Ada Lovelace: She is known as the first computer programmer because she wrote the first algorithm for a machine.
  • Alan Turing: He created the idea of a universal machine, which is the basis of modern computers.
  • Grace Hopper: She made the first compiler and helped create one of the first programming languages, COBOL.
  • John von Neumann: He designed the basic structure of most computers we use today.
  • Tim Berners-Lee: He invented the World Wide Web, which changed how we share and access information.
  • Open Source Community: Developers around the world who share their work to help others learn and create better software.
  • Modern Programming Languages: The creators of Python, JavaScript, and other languages that make coding easier and more powerful.
  • Educational Platforms: Websites like Codecademy and freeCodeCamp that teach coding to everyone for free.
  • Tech Innovators: People and companies who create new tools and technologies to make coding better and more exciting.
